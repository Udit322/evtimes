India's DC fast charger rollout is stalling — and the culprit is land, not money

Despite aggressive targets, DCFC deployment is running at 40% of planned pace. We investigated 6 months of tender data across 12 states.

Ananya Singh•11 min read•2 days ago

Fast charger deployment is increasingly bottlenecked by site access rather than capital availability. Land approvals, utility coordination, and right-of-way negotiations are delaying projects across multiple states.

That distinction matters because it changes the policy response. Subsidies and financing support help, but they cannot solve local execution friction on their own.

Operators say the most attractive sites are often the hardest to secure. As a result, rollout targets may look healthy on paper while high-demand corridors remain underserved.

Unless permitting and land-use processes improve, charger deployment could continue missing the pace required for the next wave of passenger EV growth.
